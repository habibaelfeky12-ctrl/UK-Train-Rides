

# UK Train Rides Analysis Project

## Project Overview

This project analyzes train ride data across the United Kingdom to uncover actionable insights into travel patterns, ticket pricing, route efficiency, and passenger behavior. The objective is to provide a comprehensive understanding of railway operations across different regions and time periods, supporting data-driven decision-making.

---

## Objectives

* Perform data cleaning and handle missing or inconsistent values.
* Conduct Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to identify trends in train routes, travel times, and delays.
* Analyze ticket pricing variations based on factors such as peak hours, locations, and train operators.
* Identify the busiest stations and most frequently used routes.
* Develop interactive dashboards to effectively communicate insights.

---

## Tech Stack

* **Python**: Data processing and analysis
* **Pandas & NumPy**: Data manipulation and transformation
* **Matplotlib & Seaborn**: Data visualization
* **SQL**: Database management and querying
* **Power BI / Tableau**: Dashboard development and data storytelling

---

## Key Insights

* Train travel demand is significantly higher in major metropolitan areas.
* Ticket prices vary considerably depending on time of day, with peak-hour fares being higher.
* Major hubs such as London, Manchester, and Birmingham experience the highest traffic volumes.
* Average delay times vary by region, with certain routes consistently experiencing delays.

---

## Conclusion

This analysis provides valuable insights into railway operations in the UK, highlighting key trends in demand, pricing, and performance. The findings can support stakeholders in optimizing scheduling, pricing strategies, and overall service efficiency.
